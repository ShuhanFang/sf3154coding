About this Website

Chinese cuisine is vast and profound, and due to the differences in geography, climate, customs, and specialties, a variety of dishes with local flavors and characteristics have been developed, as well as the corresponding cooking methods, resulting in the formation of different local flavors of cuisine, the most famous of which are Sichuan, Hunan, Cantonese, Fujian, Suzhou, Zhejiang, Anhui, and Lu, collectively known as the eight major cuisines.This website showcases some of the main dishes of the eight major Chinese cuisines and offers a "WHAT TO EAT TODAY?In addition, if you want to cook these dishes yourself, just click on the picture of the dish you want to cook and there are detailed steps to make it! This collection was curated by me, and all images were sourced from various online platforms.

鱼香肉丝
Shredded Pork with Garlic Sauce
Cursine: Sichuan
Prep Time:35 MINUTES
Cook Time: 5 MINUTES
1.Soak and slice wood ear mushrooms. Marinate pork strips in Shaoxing wine, oil, salt, white pepper, and cornstarch. 2.Cook pork until golden brown, then set aside. Stir-fry garlic, ginger, and doubanjiang. 3.Add carrots, wood ear mushrooms, and bamboo shoots, followed by peppers and cooked pork. 4.Pour in sauce and stir until thickened. Serve hot over steamed rice.
宫保鸡丁
SKung Pao Chicken
Cursine: Sichuan
Prep Time:25 MINUTES
Cook Time: 5 MINUTES
1.Cut chicken into 1.5-cm chunks and marinate with soy sauce, cornstarch, and oil. 2.Cook chicken in a hot wok for 20-30 seconds, add Shaoxing wine, then transfer to a plate. 3.Cook Sichuan peppercorns until dark brown, remove and set aside. 4.Add chili peppers, green onions, garlic, and ginger, then stir in a sauce. 5.Return chicken to the wok, add bell pepper and peanuts, and stir. Serve with rice and garnish with green onions
麻婆豆腐
Mapo Tofu
Cursine: Sichuan
Prep Time:10 MINUTES
Cook Time:15 MINUTES
